# Ice Dice: How to Play

This guide is based on the rule pages in `Resources/Rules_Page_1.jpg` and `Resources/Rules_Page_2.jpg`.

## Game Overview

Ice Dice is a press-your-luck game about rolling dice, collecting pyramids, and deciding when to stop before you bust out.

## Goal

Be the first player to collect **three monochrome Trios**.

## Components

- 1 Pyramid Die
- 1 Color Die
- 3 pyramids of each of the five colors for each player

For example:
- 2 players use 30 pyramids total
- 3 players use 45 pyramids total

## Setup

1. Sort all pyramids by **size** and **color**.
2. Place them off to the side where everyone can reach them. This supply is called the **Bank**.
3. Each player needs a space in front of them called their **Vault**.
4. The center of the table is the **Counter**.

## Key Terms

- **Bank**: The shared supply where all pieces begin.
- **Counter**: The central holding area where you place pieces during your turn.
- **Vault**: The area in front of each player where earned pieces are kept.

## Turn Structure

On your turn, roll both dice.

The dice tell you which pyramid to take from the Bank and place on the Counter:

- The **Color Die** determines the color.
- The **Pyramid Die** determines the size, or sometimes gives you a choice between two sizes.

Then you decide whether to keep rolling or stop.

## Choosing a Piece

Take the pyramid shown by the dice from the Bank and place it on the Counter.

Special results:

- If you roll the **Atom**, choose any color you do **not** already have on the Counter.
- If the Pyramid Die shows **two pyramids**, choose between the two sizes shown.

## Keep Rolling

You may keep rolling and adding pieces to the Counter until one of two things happens:

- You decide to stop
- You **Bust Out**

## Busting Out

You Bust Out as soon as you roll a **color that matches a piece already on the Counter**.

Important details:

- Busting Out is about **color**, not size.
- If you roll the Atom, you may choose a color that is already on the Counter, but only if you want to risk Busting Out.
- If you Bust Out, all pieces on the Counter go back to the Bank.
- You do not move anything from the Counter to your Vault.

## Rainbow Bonus

If you roll **all five colors** without Busting Out, you may move those pyramids to your Vault and immediately take another turn.

## Stealing

If the piece you roll is not in the Bank, you must steal it from an opponent’s Vault.

Rules for stealing:

- Stolen pieces stay stolen, even if you Bust Out later.
- If your roll gives you a choice, you may choose a piece that requires stealing even if other available pieces are in the Bank.
- If your Vault already contains the exact size and color you rolled, you get nothing from that roll, but you may roll again.

## Winning

You win by collecting **three monochrome Trios**.

Notes:

- Having two Trios of the same color is allowed.
- The order you collect them in does not matter.
- You can reorganize your Vault as you go.
- Extra pyramids in your Vault do not matter.

## Younger Player Variant

For a gentler version, remove the press-your-luck element:

1. Players keep rolling until they would normally Bust Out.
2. Instead of losing the Counter pieces, they simply move everything on the Counter to their Vault.
3. The Rainbow Bonus does not apply in this version.
